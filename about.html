We all have a story. This is mine.

Preface

My passion of design and strategy did not appear out of thin-air but was fostered through a career of successes and failures. I don't consider my journey to be typical in the slightest. Yet, I still look back with pride at what I was able to accomplish and with compassion for the people I was able to collaborate with.

Scroll through my time line below to see some of my milestones and moments I consider to be important in my journey.

MY JOURNEY TO NOW

2019

Moved to Pittsburgh and began attending Carnegie Mellon University for a Masters degree in Human Computer Interaction

2019

Lyda Sofa shown publicly at NeoCon in Chicago and ICFF in New York. Project has been an on going collaboration between Lauren Rottet and Haworth Contract. I worked as lead design and design consultant on this project from 2017 to 2019

2018

Launched the Fascio lighting line with Visual Comfort and Lauren Rottet in late 2018. The line consisted of 2 chandeliers, 3 different sized sconces, a floor lamp, and 3 hanging pendants. Project started in 2017 and is still on going. 

2018

Dichroic side table won best of the year design award from Interior Design magazine, Architecture magazine, and A'Design awards. I worked with Lauren Rottet to design, produce, and manufacture this product for the Rottet Collection line from early 2017 to 2018

2017

Coordinated with the Rottet Collection team to help launch the first furniture show room at NeoCon.  Showroom was deemed a huge success.

2017

Began my position as an industrial designer for Rottet Studios and Rottet Collection. Position was located in Houston, Texas.

2017

Contract ended with KBR Wyle and finished my last days at Johnson Space Center as a designer

2017

Recieved Award from Human Research Program at Johnson Space Center  for work done on the Cis Lunar habitation study. Project was done in conjuction with the Habitability Design Center

2017

Recieved accolades from the Lockheed Orion Public Affairs division for excellent work on helping build the Capsule display at Space Center Houston

2016

Began my position as a contract Industrial Designer at NASA Johnson Space Center in the Habitability Design Center. Contract was serviced through KBR Wyle by the Human Research Program in Houston, Texas. 

2016

After working with ABMG for one and a half years, I left to go persue an industrial design position at NASA as a contractor through KBR Wyle

2015

After four years of hard work, I fianlly graduated with my bachelors of industrial design from the University of Houston.

2015

Recieved an award from the Industrial Design Society of America for excellent research and product devolpment on my thesis work, Rebreathe. I also recieved a scholarship towards the project from the Make Design Matter scholarship.

2015

During my senior year of college, I began my first full time design position at AB Modern group in Houston, Texas.

2015

Group bicycle project, Tau, was featured by local Houston NPR station and Fox news station for its excellence in creativity and design.

2013

Project, Dome - modular fruit ball, was published in The International Journal of Interior Architecture and Spatial Design, Volume 1: Autonomous Identities

2011

I moved out of my home town of Plano, TX. to Houston to begin my design career studying Industrial Design at the University of Houston.

2011

Graduated from Plano East High School through the International Baccalaureate Program - reinforcing my love of literature, philosphy, art, and inclusive learning.
