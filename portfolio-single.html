Cis Lunar Habitit Study

Cis Lunar habitat was a NASA funded study to explore habitation concepts in deep space(lunar orbit) as a precursor for a Martian Transportation habitat.The project was created and assigned to the Habitability Design Center as an exploration into understanding what defines a good habitat for 4 people in a 90+ day mission. The Habitat would consist of two 4m x 4m cylinders and be launched on the ULA SLS system rockets in the late 2020's. This project was conducted over 2016 and 2017 and is still on going today at NASA.

My contribution to the project is below including research generation, concept development, and Human in the Loop diagraming. The team consisted of tow head architects, a human factors expert, and myself.

Research

At the beginning of the project, our team conducted precedent research on previous habitats to learn any pitfalls that occurred during the mission. This was done by combing through personal accounts and functional review boards created by NASA after any mission. Due to the lack of habitable space environments created, we were limited to only reviewing the ISS, MIR, and Skylab. These are some findings distilled down.

Ideation

Taking into account the precedent study and the mission directive - we began to ideate on what functional spaces can coexist in habs together. We also explored the relationship between work spaces, leisure spaces, and private spaces during multiple bubble mapping sessions.

Early programming studies were done to understand the relationship between functional space, egress, and patterns of stowage usage. Hab 1 is on the left - Hab 2 is to the right

Combined sketches - like above - were critiqued and analyzed by human factors experts as a basis point for design generation. This particular design received high feedback

Combining our precedent studies and early ideation - we were able to synthesize a star graph helping us to be more methodical in spatial programming for the habitat

With a clearer picture of the relationship between spaces and functional allocation - Sketches were created visualizing multiple early proposals

Refinement

Using 3D representations of each functional area, we created programming schemes that were detailed out to be early Augmented reality walkthroughs with astronauts. These are a couple of schemes I contributed to our team deliverable.

Alpha concept

This program focused on creating a horizontal split-level design as designated by the task lead. The programming is based off of 3 dimensional CAD blocks that represent the functional space required for certain activities. These programs helped to define the sections, elevations and floor plans used for evaluations by the core team.

Beta concept

This program focused on creating a vertical 4 level design as designated by the task lead. The programming is based off of 3 dimensional CAD blocks that represent the functional space required for certain activities. These programs helped to define the sections, elevations and floor plans used for evaluations by the core team.

Conclusion

The work that our team completed on the Cis Lunar study is still shaping the project today and setting a precedent for how the NASA internal review board will rate private industry habitat submissions. While the Cis-Lunar mission has moved from being high priority in recent years, the project will always be important to myself and the HDC team.
